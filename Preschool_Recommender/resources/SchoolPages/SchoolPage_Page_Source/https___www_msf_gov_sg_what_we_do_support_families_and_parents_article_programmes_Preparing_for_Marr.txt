                            <a href="/what-we-do/index/support-families-and-parents/marriage-journey/programmes " aria-label="Marriage Journey">Marriage Journey</a>

            <div class="article-content">
                <h2>Overview of Marriage Preparation Programmes</h2><p>The Ministry – through its network of partners – organises, supports and provides marriage education programmes to couples of various needs.</p><p>Preparing for a wedding is merely the beginning of preparing for a marriage. As much as wedding preparations take time and effort, so does marriage. A good marriage needs a good foundation and it takes commitment, effort and skills.</p><p>Marriage preparation programmes will help you understand issues relating to marriage and learn skills that help prepare for life together as husband and wife.</p><p>Tips on selecting Marriage Preparation Programmes for you and your partner:</p><ul><li>Shop Around. There are differences in the programmes offered. Do consider details such as duration of the programme, location, class size and cost.</li><li>Decide Together. Decide with your partner which programme is suitable and when would be a good time to attend.</li><li>Register directly with the service provider.
    </li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><h2>Marriage Programmes</h2><br><div class="accordion" id="accordionExample"><div id="Content_C006_Col00" class="sf_colsIn sub-section " data-sf-element="Accordion Content" data-placeholder-label="Accordion Content"><div class="accordion-item" id="item_1"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_1"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_1" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_1">Prevention and Relationship Education Programme (PREP)

It is designed based on over 30 years of research in US that aims to help soon-to-wed and newly-wed couples to:</p><ul><li>Improve communication and problem-solving skills</li><li>Attain higher relationship satisfaction; and</li><li>Develop better conflict resolution skills</li></ul><br><p>Research has shown that couples who attended PREP have higher levels of positive communication and interaction and greater confidence in the future of their marriages.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>PREP 12-hour Workshop</h3><p>Couples can also sign up for the PREP 12-hour workshop. The workshop, conducted over 2 days (weekends), provides deeper coverage on a range of topics such as communication, conflict resolution and problem-solving. Couples also have
                        opportunities during the workshop to reflect on their relationship and learn skills to enrich their marriage.</p><p><a href="https://familiesforlife.sg/discover-an-article/Documents/FFL_PREP_EDM.pdf" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">More information on this evidence-based marriage programme</a></p><p>The Ministry provides couples a <strong>$140 rebate*</strong> upon completion of the PREP 12-hour workshop.                         in their relationship skills with the help of a Certified Facilitator and the P/E assessment inventory tool.</p><p>P/E MEP is offered by the following agencies:</p><br><p><strong><a href="https://www.amkfsc.org.sg/what-we-do/family-services/fam4life" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">AMKFSC Community Services</a></strong><br>Contact No: 6351 0610<br>Email Address:

            </button></h2><div id="collapse_item_5" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>Singapore Citizen (SC)-foreigner couples are encouraged to attend marriage programmes for SC-foreigner couples to start their marriage on a strong foundation.                         may be required to attend these programmes before they are issued the LTVP. Couples are advised to attend the programmes early.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>Marriage Preparation and Marriage Support Programmes</h3><p>Benefits of programmes:</p><br><ul><li>Help SC-foreigner couples address cross-cultural issues</li><li>Help the foreign spouses better adjust and integrate into society</li><li>Link couples to available community resources</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Marriage programmes for SC-foreigner couples generally comprise a preparation component before marriage, and a support component after marriage. The programmes cover areas on strengthening couples' relationship within a cross-cultural
                        context, conflict management, managing finances and practical information about staying and living in Singapore.</p><p>To ensure that couples' specific needs are met, they can attend other customised marriage preparation courses before attending the marriage programmes for SC-foreigner couples.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>Pre-Marriage Long-Term Visit Pass Assessment (PMLA)</h3><p>SC-foreigner couples are strongly encouraged to undergo PMLA before marriage to check if you will qualify for long-term stay in Singapore after getting married.             </button></h2><div id="collapse_item_6" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>Marriage is a life-long journey. It is important to build a strong and long-lasting marriage from the start.</p><p>Marriage preparation programmes are designed to address the needs of different types of marriages. The different programmes provide an effective platform for couples to gain information and tips to strengthen their marriage.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>Programme for Minor Couples - INSPIRASI HUBs</h3><p>If either one or both of you are below 21 years old, you will have to complete a marriage preparation programme before you file for an application for marriage. You will also be required to obtain consent from your parents or guardians.
                        The changes to the minor marriage application requirements took effect from 22 October 2018.</p><p>The marriage preparation programme is offered by INSPIRASI HUBs. Through the mandatory marriage preparation programme, couples would be able to make informed decisions about the marriage and be better prepared for the next stage of
                        their journey.</p><p><a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/docs/default-source/programmes-document/amla2017---faqs-for-website.docx?sfvrsn=d30daa3b_3" target="_blank">More information on the amendments</a></p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>Programme for Young Couples</h3><p>If either you or your partner is below 21 years old, find out more about what makes a successful marriage in <a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/docs/default-source/programmes-document/marriage-preparation-programme-for-young-couples-by-inspirasi-hubs.docx?sfvrsn=f7cc2619_3" target="_blank">Marriage Preparation Programme for Young Couples by INSPIRASI HUBs.docx</a></p><p>Grooms aged 21 to 24 years old are also encouraged to attend the programmes with their partners.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>Cinta Abadi… Marriage of a Lifetime for First Marriages</h3><p>If you are entering marriage for the first time, begin your journey with <a href="https://www.cintaabadi.sg/cinta-abadi-marriage-preparation-programme" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer">Cinta Abadi Marriage Preparation Programme</a>.</p><p><a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/what-we-do/article/programmes/Cinta-Abadi-Marriage-Preparation-Programme">Cinta Abadi</a> caters to Muslim first marriages with grooms aged 25 years old and above and brides aged 21 years old and above.
                        The programme is conducted in English and Malay at various locations across the island.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><h3>Remarriage Preparation Programme</h3><p>Is this a remarriage for you or your partner? Learn how to make this marriage work and pick up tips on step-parenting and helping the children adjust to a new family in the <a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/docs/default-source/programmes-document/ppis_vista_sakinah_info_revised_feb22_final.pdf?sfvrsn=e6f20d8a_3" target="_blank">Remarriage Preparation Programme</a>.</p></div></div></div><div class="accordion-item" id="item_7"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="for-minors"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_item_7" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_item_7">Mandatory Marriage Programmes for Minors
            </button></h2><div id="collapse_item_7" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>To help minor couples better prepare for marriage, they are required to attend a marriage preparation programme before they can be issued with a marriage licence. This is in addition to obtaining parental consent.</p><p><strong>Effective for couples marrying on and after 1 October 2016</strong>, the above requirement will apply where one or both of the marrying parties are Singapore Citizens or Permanent Residents; and</p><ul><li>Where at least one party is below 18 years old; or</li><li>Where <strong><span style="text-decoration: underline">at least one</span></strong> party is aged 18 years and above but below 21 years old</li></ul><p>Note: Age is at the day of solemnisation.</p><p>Minor couples are also required to attend a marriage preparation programme <strong><span style="text-decoration: underline">conducted by a MSF-approved organisation</span></strong>.</p><p>For marriages where one party is below 18 years old, couples must also undergo social assessment by MSF to assess their suitability for marriage before they are issued with a Special Marriage Licence.</p><p><a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/docs/default-source/programmes-document/list-of-msf-approved-organisations-offering-emsa9fe84c3-bee0-4fb0-9627-2f198a6606cb.pdf?sfvrsn=d806b91e_3" target="_blank">Download the list of MSF-approved organisations that provide marriage preparation programmes for couples aged 18 to below 21 years old</a></p></div></div></div><div class="accordion-item" id="item_8"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_item_8"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_item_8" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_item_8">Premarital Counselling
            </button></h2><div id="collapse_item_8" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>Premarital counselling – often a part of marriage preparation programmes – helps couples to prepare for marriage, work through issues, resolve differences and determine if they are ready for marriage.</p><p>Couples can visit these organisations for premarital counselling services:</p><ul><li>Secular organisations such as social service agencies and private counselling centres;</li><li>Religious organisations such as churches, mosques and temples; and</li><li>Private Counsellors / family therapists</li></ul><p><a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/docs/default-source/programmes-document/rebate_web_brochure_fy2020.pdf?sfvrsn=b8923ac8_3" target="_blank">View the list of MSF-approved providers of marriage education and support programmes</a></p><p><strong>&nbsp;</strong></p><p><strong>Related links:</strong><br><a href="https://www.msf.gov.sg/what-we-do/famatfsc/home" target="_blank">Strengthening Families Programme@FSC</a><br>Find out more information on marriage preparation, support and counselling at the Strengthening
                        Families Programme@FSC ("FAM@FSC") website. FAM@FSC provides marriage and divorce support and family counselling in 10 centres across Singapore.</p></div></div></div><div class="accordion-item" id="item_9"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_item_9"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_item_9" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_item_9">Financial Planning for Couples
