<h1 class="moe-text-h1 c:grey-1">A-Level curriculum and subject syllabuses</h1>
<div class="moe-text-summary m-y:m"><p>The A-Level curriculum and subject syllabuses are designed to develop the values, skills and dispositions that students would need to take them through life as responsible adults and active citizens.</p></div>

                </div>
            </div>
        </header>

<h3>Overview</h3>
<p>The A-Level curriculum focuses on <strong>3</strong> areas, namely <strong>Life skills</strong>, <strong>Knowledge skills</strong> and <strong>Subject disciplines</strong>.</p>
<p><strong>Life skills</strong> ensure that students develop sound values, skills and dispositions to take them through life as responsible adults and citizens. It comprises the non-academic curriculum, for example:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Character and Citizenship Education, including Key Student Development Experiences such as:
    <ul>
        <li>Co-Curricular Activities</li>
        <li>Values in Action</li>
        <li>Student Leadership Development programmes</li>
    </ul>
    </li>
    <li>Physical Education</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Knowledge skills</strong> seek to develop students thinking, process and communication skills. This will enable students to analyse information and express their thoughts and ideas clearly and effectively. It comprises skills-based subjects, such as:</p>
<ul>
    <li>General Paper</li>
    <li>Knowledge and Inquiry</li>
    <li>Project Work</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Subject disciplines</strong> ensure that students have a good grounding across different areas of study. It comprises subjects in the Languages, Humanities and the Arts, and Mathematics and Sciences domains. Some examples of subjects in each domain are:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Languages
    <ul>
        <li>Mother Tongue Languages</li>
        <li>Foreign Languages</li>
    </ul>
    </li>
    <li>Humanities and the Arts
    <ul>
        <li>Art</li>
        <li>China Studies in English</li>
        <li>Economics</li>
        <li>Geography</li>
        <li>History</li>
        <li>Literature in English</li>
        <li>Management of Business</li>
        <li>Mother Tongue Language &amp; Literature</li>
        <li>Music</li>
        <li>Theatre Studies and Drama</li>
    </ul>
    </li>
    <li>Mathematics and Sciences
    <ul>
        <li>Biology</li>
        <li>Chemistry</li>
        <li>Computing</li>
        <li>Mathematics</li>
        <li>Physics</li>
        <li>Principles of Accounting</li>
    </ul>
    </li>
</ul>
<div class="moe-card moe-note moe-note">
    <div class="d:f fld:c tablet(fld:r)">

        </div>
        <div class="contain"><ul>
    <li>There may be requirements that students must meet to take certain subjects. <a href="/-/media/files/post-secondary/important-notes-on-subjects-offered-for-pre-u-course.pdf" target="_blank">Learn more about the subjects offered for the pre-university course and their requirements</a>.</li>
    <li>There are programmes offered in different schools to further develop students strengths and areas of interest. <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/education-in-sg/our-programmes/pre-university" target="_blank">Learn more about these programmes.</a></li>
    <li>Scholarships may be offered to students pursuing specific programmes. <a rel="noopener noreferrer" href="https://www.moe.gov.sg/financial-matters/awards-scholarships/programme-scholarships-pre-u" target="_blank">Learn more about these scholarships</a>.</li>
</ul></div>
