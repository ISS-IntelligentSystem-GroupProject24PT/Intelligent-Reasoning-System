<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Foundational Approach (Fundamental/Developmental)</strong></h4>
<p>The early years are when foundational developmental blocks are laid for future growth and formation of children. The body, soul and spirit of young children must be holistically nurtured within a wholesome educational environment so that they will be successful learners throughout their lives.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Multi-pathways Approach (Multiple Intelligences)</strong></h4>
<p>Each child has a unique ‘cognitive profile’ that describes the different natural intelligences of the child and impacts the way a child learns. We provide different pathways to learning that integrate the differences in the intelligences in order to help children learn more naturally.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Nurture Approach (Learning Process)</strong></h4>
<p>We provide an environment where children learn and socialise with one another with support and guidance from nurturing teachers.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Integrated Approach (Curriculum Systems)</strong></h4>
<p>We use an eclectic approach where the best of different practices are integrated.<br>
• &nbsp;Multiple Intelligences Approach (Howard Gardner, US)<br>
• &nbsp;Inquiry-based Learning – Project Approach (Lillian Katz &amp; Sylvia Chard, US)<br>
• &nbsp;Play-based Learning – hands-on activities<br>
• &nbsp;Nature-based Learning – integrating nature</p>
<p>Our curriculum materials are gleaned from various sources.<br>
• &nbsp;Chinese teaching resources from Singapore, China &amp; Taiwan<br>
• &nbsp;MATAL (Creative and Critical Thinking), Israel<br>
• &nbsp;Phonics Foundation, Singapore<br>
• &nbsp;State of the Art Program (Visual Arts), USA<br>
• &nbsp;Vygotsky’s Key to Learning, Russia</p>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<div class="contentframe">
<p>Our curriculum incorporates the most current and advanced global practices in early childhood education. These educational practices are designed to form the foundational developmental blocks found in our SPICES curriculum.SPICES (Developmental Blocks)</p>
<p>S – Spiritual<br>
<em>Character Building and Moral Development</em></p>
<p>P – Physical<br>
<em>Bodily-Kinaesthetic Development</em></p>
<p>I – Intellectual<br>
<em>Verbal-Linguistic and Logical-Mathematical Development</em></p>
<p>C – Creative Arts<br>
<em>Visual-Spatial (Artistic, Dramatic &amp; Dance) and Musical Development</em></p>
<p>E – Emotional<br>
<em>Intrapersonal Development</em></p>
<p>S – Social<br>
<em>Interpersonal Development</em></p>
</div>
</div>
</div>

<p>The Pre-Nursery programme, which is flexible and less structured, has creative play and the multiple intelligences of children as its underlying philosophical basis. Thematic activities and learning materials from <em>Key to Learning</em> all foster the different developmental areas of the children.St. James’ Church Kindergarten is the first preschool in Asia to use <em>Key to Learning</em>, an early childhood programme based on the educational and developmental theories of Vygotsky. Children will pick up language and numeracy skills, as well as social skills through an integrated process of learning through creative play.</p>
<p>Art and craft, construction, cookery, dramatic play, music and movement, outdoor activities, physical development, sand and water play, and story-telling sessions are all features of the Pre-Nursery Playgroup programme.</p>

<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">English</strong></h4>
<p>Our English programme aims to develop children’s literacy skills and competence in the language. The phonics component teaches children the basic skills in reading and spelling decodable words, as well as recognising sight words. The programme combines stories, rhymes, literacy activities, reading, spelling and creative writing. Together with our in-house materials, we use 8 readers per year from Nursery to K2.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Chinese</strong></h4>
<p>Our thematic approach helps develop children’s interest in Mandarin by building their language abilities through listening, speaking, recognising Chinese characters and reading. The programme combines stories, songs, rhymes, literacy activities, games, reading and writing, and introduces children to the Chinese culture, customs and good values.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Mathematics</strong></h4>
<p>Our Mathematics materials are from Key to Learning, an early childhood programme based on Vygotskian theories of learning.<br>
Sensory Mathematics helps the Nursery children recognise and apply fundamental sensory standards of colour, shape and size. It embeds fundamental conceptual learning in carefully structured and sequenced everyday activities. The tasks offer systematic procedures for developing foundational mathematical understanding.<br>
Mathematics introduces the Kindergarten 1 and Kindergarten 2 children to the concepts and language of mathematics and develops fundamental understanding of mathematical relationships. Children learn the concepts of number, quantity and measurement in fun and meaningful ways.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Art &amp; Craft</strong></h4>
<p>Activities like painting, printing, drawing, collage work and handcraft allow children another medium of expression that is fun and absorbing. Attention is focused on aesthetic appreciation and creativity. Besides individual art creations, children will also be involved in group art projects. There is often integration between art activities and other activities in the programme, and we use a combination of our own materials and an American programme, <b>State of the Art Program (Visual Arts)</b>.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Music &amp; Movement</strong></h4>
<p>While elements of music and movement are interwoven into the teaching of English, Chinese and Mathematics, there is a separate time set aside for the exploration of music and movement.</p>
<p>佳美音符 (Jia Mei Ying Fu) is a creative Chinese Music and Movement Programme designed and developed by Our Music Classroom (OMC) Pte Ltd. Jia Mei Ying Fu’s curricula is based on a SONG STRETCHER™ approach where songs/chants/rhymes/music are ‘s-t-r-e-t-c-h-e-d’ through vocals, body percussion, instrument play, and movement. This kinaesthetic approach helps children internalise the music process and builds their creative expression. Jia Mei Ying Fu utilises music &amp; movement as a vehicle for language development and music appreciation. Through fun and creative music and movement activities, children will be engaged and immersed in meaningful Chinese Language experiences.</p>
<p>Children will be involved in singing, movement, musical games, body percussion and appreciation of classical and multicultural songs. Elements of music such as beat, rhythm, dynamics, form and tempo are embedded in the lessons. This specialised music and language immersion curricula aims to build a strong foundation for future formal advancement in both music and language</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Creative and Critical Thinking</strong></h4>
<p>During Creative and Critical Thinking lessons, children have the opportunity to express their thoughts and ideas. It is a time where children are encouraged to ask questions and to come up with logical answers themselves. The emphasis is on the observation of the environment using all the senses. Components of the <b>MATAL Early Childhood Programme</b> from Israel form the basis in developing these critical thinking skills.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Learning Centre Activities</strong></h4>
<p>Various learning centres, including art, construction, dramatic, English, Chinese, Mathematics and library, are set up in the classrooms to provide opportunities for independent learning. Learning centres offer a variety of activities and materials that cater to the multiple intelligences of children, and children can choose to go to the learning centres that interest them.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Mini Projects &amp; Project Work</strong></h4>
<p>Every term, the children will spend a few weeks on mini projects based on the <em>Project Approach</em>. We believe that involvement in projects will enrich and add a new dimension to children’s learning which is fairly fluid in nature. Also the children play an integral part in making choices and determining the direction it takes.</p>
<p>In Term 3, the children will spend several weeks on a major project and different classes explore different topics. This project work culminates in an Open House where children present and share with their parents what they have learnt.</p>
<p><span style="color: #000000;">The <em>Project Approach&nbsp;</em> involves three phases:</span></p>
<p>Phase 1 – Brainstorming, questioning, discussing &amp; planning<br>
Phase 2 – Investigating, interviewing &amp; field trip<br>
Phase 3 – Selecting, compiling information &amp; presenting</p>

<p>While these special activities are optional, children are strongly encouraged to participate. Parents are required to give their consent and make the necessary payments for these activities.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Excursions/Field Trips</strong></h4>
<p>The children are taken on excursions or field trips two/three times a year, in Terms 2, 3 and 4. These provide enriching opportunities for children to visit places resulting in meaningful learning experiences. These trips create lasting and memorable impressions in the minds of the children.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Annual Concert</strong></h4>
<p>This is the highlight of the year and a much talked-about event. Our concerts are musicals of exceptionally high quality and are a feast for the senses. Every child in K2 is given an opportunity to participate in this exciting event.</p>
<h4><strong style="color: #993847;">Camps</strong></h4>
<p>There is a special camp for the K1 &amp; K2 children on the last day of Term 2 and a graduation camp for the K2 children on the last 2 days of Term 4.&nbsp; Children will enjoy the fun-filled as well as educational and creative camp activities.</p>
