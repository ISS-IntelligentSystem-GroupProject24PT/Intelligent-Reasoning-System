				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h3>Is there anything I can do to prepare my child for preschool before their first day?</h3>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><img decoding="async" src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/socia-dramatic-231x300.png" data-src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/socia-dramatic-231x300.png" width="231" height="300" alt="" class="wp-image-7261 alignright size-medium lazyloaded"><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/socia-dramatic-231x300.png" width="231" height="300" alt="" class="wp-image-7261 alignright size-medium lazyload" /></noscript></p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱ </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Talk to your child about starting preschool. Talk your child through what the first day of preschool will </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">be like, including the many fun things they will be doing at preschool, for example playing in the playground. Mentally prepare them for this new and exciting experience.</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> <span style="font-weight: 400;">Take your child to visit the preschool. This will allow your child to become familiar with the </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">surroundings, and maybe even a few faces around the preschool. At Shaws Preschool, we encourage new children to come and play in the playground to familiarise them with the environment (subject to safe distancing measures at place at the time).</span></p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱ </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">Let your child choose their school bag and water bottle, and </span><span style="font-weight: 400;">involve him/her in packing these things.</span></p></div>

				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h3>Tips for drop off</h3>
<p>Here are some tips we have for drop off:</p>
<p><img decoding="async" src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/creative-thinking-225x300.png" data-src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/creative-thinking-225x300.png" width="225" height="300" alt="" class="wp-image-7266 alignright size-medium lazyloaded"><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/02/creative-thinking-225x300.png" width="225" height="300" alt="" class="wp-image-7266 alignright size-medium lazyload" /></noscript></p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Drop off early or on time – while the children are enjoying free play, and before the class starts. Most children love playing outdoors!</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Drop off at the same time daily. Children love routine, so this is a great way to start the routine.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Keep your morning routine at home consistent. Again, children love routine, and this sets the tone for their day.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Be happy at drop off. Remind your child what a great day they are going to have at school. Remind your child that you will be pickng up your child at the end of the day and will want to hear all about their day.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Keep drop offs short. Children can sense any reluctance parents may have when leaving their child, so a quick goodbye lets your child settle in quickly without any</p></div>

<p>Our wonderful school bus drivers and bus “aunties” are experienced with young children. They will ensure your child has a pleasant trip on the bus to and from school.</p></div>

<p><img decoding="async" src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/6._Mastery_Play-copy-300x212.png" data-src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/6._Mastery_Play-copy-300x212.png" width="300" height="212" alt="" class="wp-image-7592 alignright size-medium lazyloaded"><noscript><img decoding="async" src="https://shaws.com.sg/wp-content/uploads/2022/03/6._Mastery_Play-copy-300x212.png" width="300" height="212" alt="" class="wp-image-7592 alignright size-medium lazyload" /></noscript></p>
<p>Most definitely! One parent is very welcome in the classroom for the first two mornings to help settle your child (this changes due to the safe distancing rules set by ECDA, and we will update you if there are any changes).</p>
<p>Each child is different, and will have a different rate of assimilation. If your child<br>requires more than two days, the teachers will discuss this with you and work<br>out the best plan for your child.</p>
<p>To help your child settle in as easily as possible, here are some useful tips.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Do arrive early, while the children are still in the outdoor playground. This is so your child has the opportunity to be comfortable in the outdoors, before moving indoors with the group.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Only 1 adult is needed to settle your child (any more may confuse or upset your child)</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> This person should ideally be the same person over the settling period.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Please allow your child to explore his/her new environment. It is fine if he/she does not join in all the activities with the rest of the children, and choose to explore the classroom. It is a new environment and we want new joiners to feel comfortable in.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Our teachers will not come too close to you and your child on your first day. Instead, they will observe parent and child behavior, which will help us get to know your child a little better.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> Our teachers will take your child from you once you leave (we do not wish to come between a child and their parent). Please be sure to tell your child when you leave and tell them when you will be back.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> During this time, the teachers and Principal will communicate with you, giving you updates and photos as to how your child is doing.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> If after you have left your child continues to cry and the teachers are unable to settle your child, they will call you to come back earlier than planned.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> It is not uncommon for it to take three weeks or more for a child to fully settle. Our teachers will do their best to encourage them to settle as quickly as possible, working closely with you.</p>
<p><span style="color: #d81f26;">✱</span> At the one-month stage we email you with our observations as to how well your child is settling in and how they are progressing at school.</p></div>

				<div class="et_pb_text_inner"><h3>What happens when it is time for me to say goodbye to my child at preschool?</h3>
<p>On the second day, depending on how your child is at school, the teacher may suggest it is time for you to say goodbye, and leave for a little while. When this happens, there will be a teacher that will be dedicated to your child, to help him/her settle in. Your child may cry when you leave; this is normal. It is important that you say goodbye and let your child know when you will be coming back (e.g. after lunchtime).</p>
<p>Once you leave, the teachers will do their best to settle and redirect your child. If your child continues to cry, we will give you a call to come back and we may try again the next day. When you do come back, do come back smiling. Your child can sense your energy and positivity. Talk about the exciting things your child did during the day.</p>
<p>When you leave, and if you prefer to wait around the school vicinity, it is best to stay out of your child’s view. This will give him/her a chance to settle in better, without distractions or interruptions, especially if he/she is doing well, participating and is engaged. This will also give your child the chance to build trust and rapport with the teachers who are comforting him/her for a smoother transition in the coming days.</p></div>

Please prepare your child (refer to the section above.) And ensure someone is available on the phone should we need to contact you for any reason, including to ask questions or clarification.</p></div>
