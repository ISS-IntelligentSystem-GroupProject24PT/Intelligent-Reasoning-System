    <h1>Parenting Programmes</h1>

    <div class="article-details">
        <div class="article-detail">
            <div class="article-topic">
                <p>
                    Topic(s):

                            <a href="/what-we-do/index/support-families-and-parents/children-and-families/programmes " aria-label="Children &amp; Families">Children &amp; Families</a>

                </p>
            </div>
            <hr>
            <div class="article-content">
                <p>MSF has appointed ten Parenting Support Providers (PSPs) to deliver parenting support services on a regional basis. PSPs work closely with schools and community partners in their allocated regions to support parents through evidence-based parenting programmes
    and referrals to services depending on parents’ needs.</p><p>The PSPs offer two evidence-based programmes – Positive Parenting Program (Triple P) and Signposts. To sign up, parents may contact the PSPs serving their child’s school or their region or approach the child’s school Teacher-In-Charge
    of Parenting Programmes.</p><br><h2>Programme Overviews</h2><div class="accordion" id="accordionExample"><div id="Content_C006_Col00" class="sf_colsIn sub-section " data-sf-element="Accordion Content" data-placeholder-label="Accordion Content"><div class="accordion-item" id="item_1"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_1"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_1" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_1">About Positive Parenting Program (Triple P)
            </button></h2><div id="collapse_1" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>The Positive Parenting Programme (Triple P) is an evidence-based programme that equips parents with techniques to promote their children’s (aged up to 16 years) psychological, social and emotional competence. Triple P is built
                        on a tiered system that provides different degrees of parental support.</p><p>MSF’s evaluation studies found that the evidence-based Positive Parenting Programme (also known as Triple P), has positive effects on parenting and child outcomes in Singapore.</p><p>About 85% of parents endorsed the programme as being relevant to their parenting needs. Parents with secondary school-going children showed a sustained 31% reduction in their emotional distress scores, and a 22% reduction in their
                        child’s problematic behavioural scores. Parents with primary school-going children sustained a 15% improvement in their parenting satisfaction and efficacy scores, and an 18 % reduction in their child’s problematic
                        behavioural scores.</p><br><div style="overflow-x: auto; word-wrap: break-word"><table class="datatable k-table"><tbody><tr><th style="vertical-align: top" data-role="resizable"><strong>Triple P Level 2 Seminar Series</strong></th><td>3 seminars of 2 hours each for parents / grandparents of primary and secondary school children for parents who are coping well with their children’s / grandchildren's needs.<br><br><p>Triple P Level 2 Seminar Series for parents / grandparents with children / grandchildren up to <span style="text-decoration: underline">12 years old</span><br>• Seminar 1 Positive parenting / grandparenting<br>• Seminar 2 Raising confident, competent children / grandchildren<br>• Seminar 3 Raising resilient children / grandchildren
                                        </p><p>Triple P Level 2 Seminar Series for parents / grandparents with teenagers up to <span style="text-decoration: underline">16 years old</span><br>• Seminar 1 Raising responsible teenagers<br>• Seminar
                                            2 Raising competent teenagers
                                            <br>• Seminar 3 Getting teenagers connected</p></td></tr><tr><th style="vertical-align: top"><strong>Triple P Level 3</strong></th><td>4 one-to-one consultation sessions for parents who may require more help with children who display mild to moderate behavioural difficulties.<br></td></tr><tr><th style="vertical-align: top" data-role="resizable"><strong>Triple P Level 4</strong></th><td>5 group sessions, followed by 3 phone consultation sessions for parents of children with high or very high levels of behavioural difficulties who may require targeted, intensive and active parenting training.<br></td></tr><tr><th style="vertical-align: top"><strong>Triple P Online (TPOL)</strong></th><td>An online programme that allows parents to complete parenting modules independently, in their own time, from a computer, tablet or smartphone. Parents have online access to the programme for 12 months. Triple P Online
                                        provides parents with a toolbox of strategies that help them to encourage positive behaviour and to prevent and manage misbehaviour.<br><br>Through this interactive course, parents can learn at their own time
                                        and pacing, and pick up simple and practical strategies to confidently manage children’s behaviour. The course consists of videos, exercises, tips and activities, suitable for parents of children and teens.<br><br>TPOL for <strong>Toddlers to Tweens </strong>(0-12 years old) Consisting of 8 modules<br>TPOL for <strong>Pre-teens and Teens </strong>(10-16 years old) Consisting of 6 modules<br><br>Each module takes
                                        around 30 minutes to complete. Parents can save their progress at any point of the module and can complete sessions at their own pace.<br></td></tr></tbody></table><br><p><a href="https://www.triplep-parenting.net/global/about-triple-p/frequently-asked-questions/" target="_blank">Click here for more information on Triple P</a></p></div></div></div></div><div class="accordion-item" id="item_2"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_item_2"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_item_2" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_item_2">About Signposts (for Primary Schools Only)
            </button></h2><div id="collapse_item_2" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>Signposts is an evidence-based programme that equips parents with skills to help them understand, prevent and manage their child’s difficult behaviour through the development of a personalised programme that suits the unique
                        needs of their child. Parents are also taught to develop strategies to prevent and manage difficult behaviour.</p><p>It consists of 5 weekly <strong>group sessions</strong> x 2.5-hours/session.</p></div></div></div></div><div class="accordion-item" id="item_3"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_item_3"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_item_3" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_item_3">About Purposeful Play
            </button></h2><div id="collapse_item_3" class="accordion-collapse collapse" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample"><div class="accordion-body"><p>Developed jointly by the Ministry of Social and Family Development and the Parenting Research Centre from Australia, the Purposeful Play programme seeks to help parents of children aged 0-6 years develop their child through play and daily
                    interactive activities.</p><p>The Purposeful Play programme introduces you to 10 evidence-based strategies that equip you with the knowledge and skills to:</p><ul><li>Think about ways to create a warm and stimulating home environment for your child</li><li>Use everyday activities to develop language and problem-solving skills to enhance your child's school readiness, as well as to bond and strengthen your relationship with your child.</li></ul><div><p>You'll gain confidence in your skills to support your child's development and build supportive relationships with other parents.</p><p>Session 1: Warm and Gentle and Play</p><p>Session 2: Tuning In and Routines</p><p>Session 3: Following Your Child’s Lead and Shared Reading</p><p>Session 4: Listening and Talking More and Watching Screens Together</p><p>Session 5: Teachable Moments and Community Resources</p><p>It consists of&nbsp;<strong>5 in-person sessions x 1.5-hours/session with your child</strong>, conducted over 5 consecutive weeks.</p><p>For enquiries, contact us at&nbsp;<a href="mailto:parentingsupport@msf.gov.sg">parentingsupport@msf.gov.sg</a>.</p></div></div></div></div></div><br><h2>Programme Sign-Ups</h2><div class="accordion" id="accordionExample"><div id="Content_C006_Col00" class="sf_colsIn sub-section " data-sf-element="Accordion Content" data-placeholder-label="Accordion Content"><div class="accordion-item" id="item_4"><h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_item_4"><button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_item_4" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_item_4">How do I Sign-Up for Triple P Level 2 Seminars?
