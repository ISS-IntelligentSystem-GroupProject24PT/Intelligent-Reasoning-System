– Optional activities: Applicable from N1/N2 and older, strictly optional, cost varies with programme.<br>

				<div class="et_pb_toggle_content clearfix">– Only 1 adult will be needed to settle your child (any more may confuse or upset your child).<br>
– This person should preferably be the same person over the settling period and is welcome to stay for a few days either for a few hours or for the whole morning.<br>
– It is common for it to take three weeks or more for a child to fully settle in, but our teachers will do their best to encourage them to settle as quickly as possible.<br>
– At the one-month stage we email you with our observations as to how well your child is settling in.<br>
– Also, at the point you leave your child in the classroom with their new friends and teachers. We will set up a Whatsapp chat with you to show you how your child is doing.<br>
– Before you join we will request for you to complete an ‘all about me’ form, giving us lots of details about your child so we are able to help comfort and distract them if they become unsettled.</div>

				<div class="et_pb_toggle_content clearfix">If the 24 hour PSI reading is between 91 and 191, we will:<br>
– Cancel all outdoor activities for the children<br>
– Reduce physical indoor activities and exercises for children<br>
– Monitor the health situation of all children closely<p></p>
<p>If the 24 hour PSI reading is above 200, we will:<br>
– Cancel all indoor and outdoor physical activities for children<br>
– Keep children strictly indoors, close all doors and windows<br>
– Continue to monitor the health situation of all children closely</p>
<p>If the 24 hour PSI reading is above 300, we have been advised to:<br>
– No outdoor activities<br>
– Scale down on all indoor activities for children<br>
– Keep children strictly indoors, close all doors and windows<br>
– Continue to monitor the health and situation of all children closely</p>
<p>Please note that despite the PSI index, should we feel that the air is bad, we will keep the children indoors. Whatever the PSI reading, children who have respiratory conditions or any child who experiences discomfort may request to remain indoors.</p></div>
