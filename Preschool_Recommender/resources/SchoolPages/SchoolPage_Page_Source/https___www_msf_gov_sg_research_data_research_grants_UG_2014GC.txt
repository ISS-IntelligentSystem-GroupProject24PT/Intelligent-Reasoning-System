<h1>Social and Family Research Fund</h1><p><br></p><h2>Aim</h2><p>As our society evolves and as our families adapt to the rapid changes around them, it is critical for policymakers to continuously reassess policies, identify and address needs, leverage on opportunities, and prepare for the future.</p><p>The MSF Social and Family Research Fund (SFRF) was introduced in 2008 to support research which identifies emerging trends and issues, strengthen the evidence base for social and family development policies and improve the Ministry’s forward planning
    capabilities to respond quickly and effectively to new challenges and opportunities. By providing funding support to researchers, the Ministry aims to promote ground-up research initiatives which can potentially inform and make a difference to Singapore’s
    social and family landscape.</p><p><br></p><h2>Grant Categories</h2><p>The MSF Social and Family Research Fund offers two grant categories:</p><p><br></p><ul><li><a href="#undergrad" data-sf-ec-immutable="">Undergraduate Category</a>&nbsp; [The 16th Grant Call is now closed]</li><br><li><a href="#postgrad" data-sf-ec-immutable="">Post-graduate / Academic Category</a>&nbsp; [Launch date to be announced]</li></ul><p><br></p><p><strong>The Undergraduate Category of the 16<sup>th</sup> Grant Call has closed. <br></strong></p><p>&nbsp;</p><hr><p><br></p><h2 id="undergrad">Undergraduate</h2>
<div class="accordion" id="accordionExample">
    <div id="Content_C009_Col00" class="sf_colsIn sub-section " data-sf-element="Accordion Content" data-placeholder-label="Accordion Content">

Proposals with a primary focus on healthcare/medical research will not be funded, as the SFRF is meant to support research with direct relevance to MSF's areas of work.</p><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Areas include:</p><ul><li>Financial support for lower-income</li><li>Residential &amp; shelter support for the vulnerable (e.g., rough sleepers, destitute persons)</li><li>Protection from domestic violence</li><li>Youth Rehabilitation</li><li>Fostering &amp; Adoption</li><li>Supporting marriage and parenthood</li><li>Preschool &amp; Early Intervention services</li><li>Supporting Persons with Disabilities</li><li>Women Empowerment</li><li>Strengthening of the Social Service Sector</li></ul>

    <div class="accordion-item" id="funding">
        <h2 class="accordion-header" id="heading_funding">
            <button class="accordion-button collapsed" type="button" data-bs-toggle="collapse" data-bs-target="#collapse_funding" aria-expanded="true" aria-controls="collapse_funding" aria-label="Funding">
                Funding
            </button>
        </h2>
        <div id="collapse_funding" class="accordion-collapse collapse" aria-labelledby="heading_funding" data-bs-parent="#accordionExample">
            <div class="accordion-body">
                <p>Successful applicants will:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Receive a grant of up to S$1,000 per research project from MSF</li>
    <li>Sign a Research Agreement with MSF on the terms of funding</li>
</ul><br>
<p><span style="text-decoration:underline;">Stages of funding</span></p><br>
<ul>
    <li>After the signing of the Research Agreement, MSF will disburse 70% of the approved grant.</li>
    <li>MSF will reimburse the remaining amount based on actual expenditure following the Secretariat's assessment of:</li>
    <ol>
        <li>Final report approved by applicant's supervisor (within 2 months after project completion).</li>
        <li>Primary research output (including questionnaires and anonymised research output, e.g. datasets, interview transcripts, electronic resources).</li>
        <li>Progress report on the research project, including all receipts and supporting documents.</li>
    </ol>
    <li>Funding that has been disbursed but was not used upon the completion of the research project must be returned to MSF.</li>
</ul><br>
<p><span style="text-decoration:underline;">Funding terms</span></p>
<p>The grant should be used exclusively for conducting the research project, including:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Transport cost incurred during local fieldwork</li>
    <li>Incentives or tokens to research respondents</li>
    <li>Printing cost of survey questionnaires and other fieldwork materials</li>
    <li>Any other items necessary for conducting the research</li>
</ul><br>
<p>The grant <span style="text-decoration:underline;">does not cover</span> expenses such as:</p>
<ul>
    <li>Tuition fees and other administrative charges imposed by the universities as part of the course of study</li>
    <li>Overseas research travel expenses</li>
    <li>Purchase of hardware or equipment</li>
    <li>Purchase of software and license</li>
    <li>Cost of printing and binding thesis report for submission to institution</li>
    <li>Refreshments for meetings</li>
</ul><br>
<p>MSF will fund only expenditures incurred <span style="text-decoration:underline;">after</span> the signing of the Research Agreement with the successful applicants.</p>
<p>Applicants should secure approval of their research proposals and the endorsement to apply for the grant from their project supervisor at the application stage</p>
<p>Upon successful application, recipients should obtain clearance from the Institution’s Research Ethics Review Board <span style="text-decoration:underline;">before</span> any funds can be disbursed.</p>
<p>Successful applicants shall not receive any research funding from other agencies. Changes to the research project cannot be made without prior written consent of MSF.</p>

</p><ul><li><em>Yamagata Chihiro</em><br>National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Topic:<strong> Childcare Staff’s and Parents’ Beliefs about Quality Care for Infants and Toddlers in Centre-based Programs in Singapore</strong></p><p>The study aims to examine (i) Perceptions of childcare staff on quality care in terms of their interactions and practices with infants and toddlers in centre-based programmes in Singapore; (ii) Perceptions of childcare staff on the training which they
    had undergone to prepare them for their interactions and practices with infants; and (iii) Observed nature of childcare staff’s interactions and practices.</p><ul><li><em>Karuppiah Nirmala</em><br>Department of Early Childhood and Special Needs Education, National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University</li></ul><p id="PGA_2008GC" style="text-align:right;font-size:16px;"><a href="#PGA_PastRecipients">Back to top</a></p><h2>2008 Grant Call</h2><p>Topic:<strong> The Influence of Child Temperament, Parenting, and Family Context on Psychological Adaptation during Middle Childhood</strong></p><p>The study aims to outline a programme of research that focuses on the psychological development of children aged seven to 10 years, in the context of the family. The parents of children in this age range have huge influences on their children’s
    development through the manner in which they interact with one another. A major theme of the research is to delineate the factors responsible for the children’s ability to adapt well to changes, to build up psychological resilience, and to function
    successfully at home and in school.</p><ul><li><em>Ryan Hong Yee-Shiun</em><br>Department of Psychology, National University of Singapore</li><li><em>Tsai Fen-Fang</em><br>Department of Psychology, National University of Singapore</li><li><em>Tan Seok Hui</em><br>Department of Psychology, National University of Singapore</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Topic:<strong> Not Just Babysitting: Case Studies of Caregiver-Child Interactions in Infant Care Centres in Singapore</strong></p><p>The study aims to investigate the best practices of infant-toddler child care centres in Singapore, with regard to designing and implementing their programmes, with a focus on examining the interactions between child and caregiver, the environment set
    up that supports early learning and development and professional development of the edu-carers.</p><ul><li><em>Cynthia Lim Ai Ming</em><br>Department of Early Childhood, National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Topic:<strong> Impact of Older Adults’ Activity Patterns on the Functioning of Later-life Family</strong></p><p>The study aims to explore the range of meaningful activities that older adults are engaged in; to classify their activity patterns; to examine factors associated with these activity patterns; and to investigate the impact of activity patterns on older
    adults’ well-being and their family functioning. Family functioning will be assessed in four dimensions of family life which includes family adaptability, cohesion, satisfaction with family life, and family leisure life.</p><ul><li><em>Hong Song-Iee</em><br>Department of Social Work, National University of Singapore</li><li><em>Han Chang-Keun</em><br>Department of Social Work, National University of Singapore</li><li><em>Alexander Lee Earn Yung</em><br>Department of Social Work, National University of Singapore</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Topic:<strong> Longitudinal Study of Beyond Parental Control (BPC) cases: Factors Associated with Possible Variation of Court Orders</strong></p><p>The study aims to study the Beyond Parental Control (BPC) cases longitudinally over a period of two years to identify factors associated with variation of initial court orders. The researchers seek to study the BPC cases that have either been sent to
    an approved home or placed under closed statutory supervision, as these are the two most common types of court orders for BPC cases.</p><ul><li><em>Rebecca Ang Pei-Hui</em><br>School of Humanities &amp; Social Sciences, Nanyang Technological University</li><li><em>Vivien Huan Swee Leng</em><br>Psychological Studies Academic Group, National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University</li><li><em>Chong Wan Har</em><br>Psychological Studies Academic Group, National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University</li><li><em>Yeo Lay See</em><br>Psychological Studies Academic Group, National Institute of Education, Nanyang Technological University</li><li><em>Carol Balhetchet</em><br>Youth Service Centre (Toa Payoh), Singapore Children’s Society</li></ul><p>&nbsp;</p><p>Topic:<strong> A Study of the Impact of Caring for Stroke Survivors on Family Structure and the Mental Health of Caregivers</strong></p><p>The study aims to examine the factors that influence caregiving burden, quality of life and mental health of caregivers; and to examine the adequacy of existing intervention programmes within the community for stroke caregivers.</p><ul><li><em>David Matchar</em><br>Duke-NUS Medical School</li><li><em>Stella Quah</em><br>Duke-NUS Medical School</li><li><em>Edward Menon</em><br>Saint Andrew’s Community Hospital</li><li><em>Chow Wai Leng</em><br>SingHealth Centre for Health Services and Research</li><li><em>Angelique Chan</em><br>Department of Sociology, National University of Singapore</li></ul><p style="text-align:right;font-size:16px;"><a href="#PGA_PastRecipients">Back to top</a></p>
