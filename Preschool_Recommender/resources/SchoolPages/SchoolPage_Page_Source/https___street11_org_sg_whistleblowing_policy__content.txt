class="elementor-widget-container"><h3>Child at Street 11 Whistleblowing Policy and Procedure</h3></div></div><div class="elementor-element elementor-element-a3adf86 elementor-widget-divider--view-line elementor-widget elementor-widget-divider" data-id="a3adf86" data-element_type="widget" data-widget_type="divider.default"><div class="elementor-widget-container"><div class="elementor-divider"> <span class="elementor-divider-separator"> </span></div></div></div><div class="elementor-element elementor-element-b199a05 elementor-widget elementor-widget-text-editor" data-id="b199a05" data-element_type="widget" data-widget_type="text-editor.default"><div class="elementor-widget-container"><p><strong>Overview&nbsp;</strong></p><p>Child at Street 11 (the “Centre”) is committed to strong corporate governance and promotes&nbsp; an open and transparent culture. Employees, vendors, parents, children under the care of the&nbsp; Centre and other stakeholders are provided an avenue to express concerns on any serious&nbsp; wrongdoing/ malpractice in particular in relation to fraud, controls and ethics.&nbsp;</p><p>This Whistleblowing Policy and Procedures (the “Policy”) is intended to provide a framework&nbsp; to promote responsible and secure whistleblowing without fear of adverse consequences.&nbsp; Whistleblower may use the procedures set out in the Policy to report any concern or&nbsp; complaint regarding questionable accounting or auditing matters, internal controls,&nbsp; inappropriate behavior of teachers and staff in handling the children under the care of the&nbsp; Centre, conflict of interest, or any other matters involving fraud, corruption and employee&nbsp; misconduct.&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Reporting Procedure&nbsp;</strong></p><p>Concerns have to be raised in writing or in-person to the Chairman of the Audit Committee (the “AC”). Emails can be sent to <a href="mailto:risk@street11.org.sg">risk@street11.org.sg</a> or <a href="mailto:audit@street11.org.sg">audit@street11.org.sg</a>. For in person reporting, the Chairman of AC is to follow up with a written report of the issue raised&nbsp; and have the concurrence of the whistleblower before a case is logged.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p>To effectively evaluate and investigate the issue raised, all critical information relating to the&nbsp; issue have to be provided and be as specific as possible. Key information includes details of&nbsp; the parties involved, dates or period of time, the type of concern, evidence substantiating the&nbsp; complaint, where possible, and contact details, in case further information is required.&nbsp;&nbsp;</p><p><strong>Management of Whistleblowing Incidents&nbsp;</strong></p><p>All instances of whistleblowing will be logged including anonymous with factual concerns or&nbsp; complaints and independently reviewed by the Chairman of AC. Where necessary, the issue&nbsp; is to be investigated by an Investigative Committee, which is led by a member of the AC appointed by its Chairman. Results of investigation will also be reported to the AC.&nbsp;</p><p>The whistleblower’s identity and the concerns raised will be kept confidential, unless as&nbsp; required by the law to reveal to parties such as lawyers, the police or investigators.&nbsp;</p><p>Chairman of AC is to report any whistleblowing incident to the Board at the conclusion of the&nbsp; investigation. Any follow-up action is to be discussed and decided by the Board.</p></div></div></div></div></div></section></div></div></article></main></div></div></div><div class="hfe-before-footer-wrap"><div
